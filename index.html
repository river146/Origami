Interesting facts about Camels

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  • Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds.

Interesting facts about Chameleons

  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.
  • Chameleons can move their eyes separately, with each eye having a field of 180 degrees. This means they can see 360 degrees if they need to!
  • There are more than 200 species of chameleons.

Interesting facts about Pigeons

  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals

Interesting facts about Teddy Bears

  • The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.

Interesting facts about Pandas

  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • An adult panda weight more than 45 kilos (100 pounds), and can be about 1.5 meters (5 feet) long.
  • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day

Interesting facts about Cicadas

  • There are around 3,000 cicada species.
  • Their wings are transparent and can seem rainbow-hued when held up to a light source.
  • Cicadas can be black, brown or green and can have red, white or blue eyes.
